No

Reliability is a long term effort
  • Reliability is a (hidden) cost that needs to be actively managed
  • Real world experience is critical
  • "hans't crashed yet" fallacy

  • Prepare
  • Handle disasters
  • Learn and improve

Be honest with yourself

  • Relax business requirements and goals
  • Simplify the system
  • Prove your assumptions and definitions
  • Reliablity is about relying and believing
  • Actual data is hard to come by when dealing with one-of-a-kind system and rare events
  • Biases - what was is what will be

Practice and plan

  • Production visibility
  • Decision lines
  • Improve rarely used skills
  • Your telemetry might not provide the information you need
  • Are you comfortable and productive with the tools?
  • Build runbooks - miss less and be time effective

Simulate disasters

  • Systems ehibit different behaviour in stress
  • Some bugs/behaviours manifest in abnormal conditions

Faulty fallback syndrom

Improve your processes

  • Common is better
  • Indipendent is better
  • Contained is better
  • Simple is better
  • And it's better if it's cheap too

Can the system survive you?

  • things always happen at 3am

Human activity is a significant factor

  • Standartize
  • Consolidate
  • Use the same tools in dev and production
  • Manage team fatigue and wear
  • Activity, not always error
  • You are a part of the system workflow
  • Is there a fallback for you?
  • e.g. Can you work without your laptop?
