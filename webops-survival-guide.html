The fan

The sh!t

This is the part we don't control

Rare, unique incidents

  • Rare is relative, even hourly is rare in a system with 1m rpm

You

Reliability is a long term effort
  • Reliability is a (hidden) cost that needs to be actively managed
  • Real world experience is critical
  • "hans't crashed yet" fallacy

Be honest with yourself

  • Relax business requirements and goals
  • Simplify the system
  • Prove your assumptions and definitions
  • Reliablity is about relying and believing
  • Actual data is hard to come by when dealing with one-of-a-kind system and rare events
  • Biases - what was is what will be

Can the system survive you?

Human activity is a significant factor

  • Standartize
  • Consolidate
  • Use the same tools in dev and production
  • Manage team fatigue and wear
  • Activity, not always error
  • You are a part of the system workflow
  • Is there a fallback for you?
  • e.g. Can you work without your laptop?

Practice and plan

  • Production visibility
  • Decision lines
  • Improve rarely used skills
  • Your telemetry might not provide the information you need
  • Are you comfortable and productive with the tools?
  • Build runbooks - miss less and be time effective

Simulate disasters

  • Systems ehibit different behaviour in stress
  • Some bugs/behaviours manifest in abnormal conditions

It ain't paranoya when they are really out to get you

Everything fails

You can't even trust yourself

  • Tests
  • Runtime validation
  • Change management
  • Every minute million things try to break your system
  • Everything can fail
  • Heck, even I'm actively pushing bugs

Improve your processes

  • Common is better
  • Indipendent is better
  • Contained is better
  • Simple is better
  • And it's better if it's cheap too

The heat is on

This is how management feels

This is how Ops should feel

An incident is an opportunity

  • Your systems is being tested in ways you never imagined
  • That data is pricesless
  • New operational paths open

Don't panic

Collect data

Data needed for debugging, simulating and post mortem

Assumptions are the mother of all f*ckups

Never act without evidence

  • More often then not it's better not to do anything then to shoot in the dark

Do one thing at a time

Communicate!

  • Chat services
  • Log the chat
  • No invitation needed

Don't loose control

  • Designate an incident manager
  • Watch the clock and your decision lines

Prepare the handoff

Boss battle!

Take the pressure off

  • Give Ops the feeling they are not the last line
  • Mitigate business pressure
  • Stay out of Ops' hair
